WHAT IS THE ENVIRONMENT

The environment is everything around us: the air we breathe, the water we drink, the soil under our feet, the plants and animals we share this planet with, and even the social and cultural systems we live within. It’s the totality of physical, biological, and chemical factors that interact to make life possible.

Air: The mixture of gases that surrounds the planet and supports life.

Water: Oceans, rivers, lakes, and ground water. All essential for living organisms.

Land: Soil, minerals, and terrain that support plant life and ecosystems.

Living organisms: Every plant, animal, microbe, and human.

WHY IT MATTERS

A healthy environment:
Provides essentials: air, water, food.
Regulates climate and weather.
Supports biodiversity, the variety of life.
Maintains ecosystem services we depend on: pollination, fresh water, soil fertility, and more.
Without a stable environment, life as we know it collapses.

ENVIRONMENTAL THREATS

Pollution

Any substance or energy that harms natural systems is a pollutant.

Air: Emissions from factories and vehicles disrupt air quality.
Water: Chemicals and waste harm aquatic life.
Soil: Toxic substances reduce fertility and enter the food chain.

Deforestation

Cutting forests destroys habitats, reduces oxygen production, and accelerates climate change by disrupting ecosystems.

Climate Change

Burning fossil fuels raises global temperatures, causing extreme weather, rising seas, and ecosystem collapse.

Resource Over-exploitation

Mining, overfishing, and excessive consumption deplete finite resources and leave environments barren.

Loss of Biodiversity

When species disappear, ecosystems weaken and lose their ability to recover from environmental stress.

SAVING THE ENVIRONMENT

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Minimize waste. Don’t default to single-use plastics. Recycle materials like paper, metal, and glass.

Conserve Water and Energy

Fix leaks, take shorter showers, and use energy-efficient appliances. Turn off lights and devices when you’re not using them.

Choose Sustainable Transportation

Walk, bike, carpool, or use public transport. Reducing fuel use cuts emissions immediately.

Support Sustainable Food Systems

Eat local, reduce meat consumption, and avoid food waste. These steps significantly reduce environmental strain.
